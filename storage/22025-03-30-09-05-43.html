The ethylene gas emitted from these fruits encourages faster ripening, turning hard mangos into ready-to-eat delights in no time. For long-term storage, consider freezing sliced mango. Slice and peel the mango, laying the pieces flat on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. After they’re frozen solid, transfer them to a resealable freezer bag. These cheerful fellows have a habit of ripening quickly. If you want to slow the process, try wrapping the stems of the bunch in plastic wrap or foil. This minimizes the ethylene gas concentration near the rest of the fruit. Another nifty trick is to store bananas away from other fruits, as they can accelerate ripening. Need to preserve those bananas a bit longer? Peel and slice them, then lay the slices on a parchment-lined baking sheet to freeze. Once frozen, store them in a freezer bag.
